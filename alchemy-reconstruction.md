## Series 3: Creating Artificial Gems

In this series, we recreate formulations from a thirteenth- to fifteenth-century alchemical treatise, the "Jewel Mine of Mercury" (*Rasaratnākara*). In its *Vādakhaṇḍa*, chapter 19, this treatise describes the making of gems, including rubies, sapphires, and emeralds. We explore the creation of artificial rubies.

## Series 1: The *Rasahṛdayatantra*

Indian alchemy — Rasashastra — is the craft of making elixirs from mercury. From the tenth century, Indian alchemists started composing treatises about their work. The earliest of these treatises was the 'Heart of Mercury' (*Rasahṛdayatantra*). It describes eighteen procedures for making mercurial elixirs. We reconstructed these procedures.

<div class="alchemy-section">
  <h3>Introductory Interview</h3>
  <p>Dagmar Wujastyk and Andrew Mason speak about their collaboration on recreating alchemical procedures described in this tenth-century treatise.</p>
  <div class="video-wrap">
    <iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/0fUUnqrKLd4" allowfullscreen loading="lazy"></iframe>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="alchemy-section">
  <h3>Step 1 — Steaming (svedana)</h3>
  <p>The first procedure: steaming (<em>svedana</em>) of mercury to remove contaminants. The text states: "The steaming of mercury is carried out with a sixteenth each of mustard, salt, the three pungent substances, leadwort, fresh ginger and radish together with sour gruel for three days over a mild fire." (<em>Rasahṛdayatantra</em>, chapter 2, verse 3)</p>
  <div class="video-wrap">
    <iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/jM7sg4LCKaM" allowfullscreen loading="lazy"></iframe>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="alchemy-section">
  <h3>Step 2 — Trituration (mardana)</h3>
  <p>The second procedure: trituration (<em>mardana</em>) of mercury with several other substances to remove contaminants. The text states: "Trituration with sour gruel is carried out for three days with molasses, burnt wool, and salt, together with soot, brickdust, and mustard, each in the amount of a sixteenth part to the mercury." (<em>Rasahṛdayatantra</em>, chapter 2, verse 4)</p>
  <div class="video-wrap">
    <iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/BIxhcwMwCvU" allowfullscreen loading="lazy"></iframe>
  </div>
  <p><strong>Preliminary preparation: Calcining wool</strong> — production of burnt wool, an ingredient in the trituration procedure.</p>
  <div class="video-wrap">
    <iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/aJBliUyKpwE" allowfullscreen loading="lazy"></iframe>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="alchemy-section">
  <h3>Step 3 — Thickening (mūrchā)</h3>
  <p>The third procedure: thickening (<em>mūrchā</em>) to remove toxic elements from mercury. The text states: "The three inherent faults of mercury are called 'dirt', 'fire', and 'poison'. Through dirt, it produces fainting, through fire, a burning sensation, through poison, death. Aloe removes dirt, the three myrobalans remove fire, and leadwort removes poison. Therefore, one should thicken it seven times with a mixture of these." (<em>Rasahṛdayatantra</em>, chapter 2, verses 5–6)</p>
  <div class="video-wrap">
    <iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/IddH7TmxdcU" allowfullscreen loading="lazy"></iframe>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="alchemy-section">
  <h3>Step 4 — Raising (utthāpana)</h3>
  <p>The fourth procedure: 'raising' or sublimation (<em>utthāpana</em>) of mercury to activate it after the thickening process. The text states: "The mercury, which has become completely cleansed and freed from lead and tin through trituration with this, is brought to a rise from a sour gruel decoction in a condensation apparatus." (<em>Rasahṛdayatantra</em>, chapter 2, verse 7)</p>
  <div class="video-wrap">
    <iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/sQ7OqbJXkCk" allowfullscreen loading="lazy"></iframe>
  </div>
  <p><strong>Preliminary preparation: Making egg tempera to seal the apparatus.</strong> Since clay is a porous material, it needs to be sealed before use in procedures. We used egg tempera following instructions from later alchemical texts.</p>

    <iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/KHsD72vRe9Q" allowfullscreen loading="lazy"></iframe>

<div class="alchemy-section">
  <h3>Step 5 — Letting Fall (pātana)</h3>
  <p>The fifth procedure: "letting fall" or sublimation (<em>pātana</em>) to fully remove toxic lead and tin from mercury. The text states: "Having prepared a copper paste, a condensation procedure is conducted because of suspicion of remaining lead and tin. And, thus freed of its faults, the cleansed mercury condensates." (<em>Rasahṛdayatantra</em>, chapter 2, verse 8)</p>

<div class="alchemy-section">
  <h3>Step 6 — Reviving (nirodhā)</h3>
  <p>The sixth procedure: 'reviving' (<em>nirodhā</em>) of mercury to reinvigorate it after the cleansing procedures. The term <em>nirodhā</em> normally means 'restraint', but here is understood as a synonym for <em>bodhana</em>, 'awakening'. The text states: "It is rendered useless through trituration, thickening and condensation because of its weak potency. Once it has become replenished through revivification with sṛṣṭyambuja (water and salt?), it will no longer be impotent." (<em>Rasahṛdayatantra</em>, chapter 2, verse 9)</p>
