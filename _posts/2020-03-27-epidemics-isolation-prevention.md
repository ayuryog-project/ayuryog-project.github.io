At the time of writing, nearly half of the world's population is under some form of movement restriction due to the COVID-19 coronavirus outbreak — a situation without precedent in living memory.

The classical āyurvedic literature, however, is no stranger to epidemics. The *Carakasaṃhitā* and the *Suśrutasaṃhitā* both contain detailed discussions of *janapadodhvaṃsa* — the devastation of communities by epidemic disease — and prescribe responses that include isolation, purification of the environment, and the use of specific herbs and formulations. The *Aṣṭāṅgahṛdayasaṃhitā* similarly addresses the conditions under which epidemic disease arises and the measures appropriate to different types of outbreak.

This post reflects on what these classical sources say about epidemics, isolation, and preventive medicine, and asks what historical perspectives might offer to contemporary discussions.
