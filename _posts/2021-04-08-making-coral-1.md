New series! After completing our reconstruction of the eighteen procedures for treating mercury from the *Rasahṛdayatantra*, we turn to the *Rasaprakāśasudhākara*, a sixteenth-century treatise, and its recipes for artificial gems and precious materials.

We begin with artificial coral. The recipe calls for purified conch shell ground to a fine powder, combined with other ingredients and processed with heat to produce a red, coral-like material. The first attempt gave us useful data about what the text means — but the colour and texture were not yet right.
