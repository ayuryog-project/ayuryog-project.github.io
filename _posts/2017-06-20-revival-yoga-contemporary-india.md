The 21st of June was declared by the United Nations as 'International Yoga Day' (first celebrated in 2015). During this period of international celebrations of yoga, it is particularly valuable to recall the complex and multi-valent nature of yoga in the contemporary world.

Yoga in contemporary India is not a simple continuation of an ancient tradition. It has been shaped by nationalist movements, colonial encounters, transnational exchange, and commercial interests — as well as by genuine spiritual aspiration and ongoing scholarly and practical engagement with classical sources. Understanding this complexity is essential for both scholars and practitioners.
