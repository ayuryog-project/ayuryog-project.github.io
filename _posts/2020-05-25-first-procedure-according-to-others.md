Perhaps you noticed at the end of the first AyurYog film on reconstructing alchemical procedures that the filmmaker thanks Dr Jinal Thakkar and Dr Parth Kale. These are the rasaśāstra experts with formal university training in India that Andrew Mason has been consulting throughout the project.

It is important to our methodology that we do not work in isolation from living practitioners and scholars trained in the rasaśāstra tradition. Their knowledge of the materials and procedures — grounded in hands-on training rather than purely textual study — provides a crucial check on our philological interpretations and flags practical problems we might otherwise miss.

In this post we compare our reconstruction of the first procedure with accounts from other researchers who have attempted the same recipes, and reflect on what the differences tell us.
