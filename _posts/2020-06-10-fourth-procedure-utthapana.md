The mercury, still thickened from the preceding *mūrchā* procedure, will now be brought back to its more familiar shine and mobility with the fourth procedure: *utthāpana*, 'bringing to a rise' — that is, letting mercury evaporate and then condense in its cleansed form.

The condensation device (*pātanayantra*) used for this procedure is described only in general terms by the *Rasahṛdayatantra*. We followed construction instructions from later alchemical texts, building the device from clay sealed with egg tempera.
