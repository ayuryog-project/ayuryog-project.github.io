*Originally published in The Conversation.*

Āyurvedic medicine understands the human body in ways that differ substantially from modern biomedical models. Where biomedicine focuses on organs, cells, and biochemical processes, āyurveda centres on the three *doṣas* (vāta, pitta, and kapha), the seven *dhātus* (bodily tissues), and the concept of *agni* (digestive fire) as the mediator between food and body.

How do we make sense of these different frameworks? This post draws on the history and sociology of medicine to ask how āyurvedic and biomedical understandings of the body relate to each other — and what the encounter between them has produced in both directions.
