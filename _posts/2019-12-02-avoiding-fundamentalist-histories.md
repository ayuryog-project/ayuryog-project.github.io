An important theme of AyurYog research has been the complexity of entanglements. In the past few years we have been able to shine a spotlight on a few areas of intersection between yoga, ayurveda and *rasaśāstra* (Indian alchemy).

This post reflects on the project's research in the context of the launch of *Yoga in Britain: Stretching Spirituality and Educating Yogis* (2019), a book by Suzanne Newcombe examining the history of yoga in Britain from the late nineteenth century to the present. The book argues against "fundamentalist" histories that present yoga as an unchanging ancient tradition, and for a more nuanced account that takes historical complexity seriously.
