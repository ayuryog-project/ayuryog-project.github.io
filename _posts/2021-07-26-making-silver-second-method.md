Out of all the recipes we chose, this one probably had us scratching our heads the most: the *Rasaprakāśasudhākara*'s second recipe for making silver. It features uncertain and exotic materials, and an unusual method.

The recipe calls for a piece of *sumalakṣāraka* the weight of a *karṣa*, wrapped in human hair, immersed in molten lead, and then quenched twenty-one times in lemon juice. What exactly *sumalakṣāraka* is remains debated — the term does not appear widely in other alchemical sources. Identifying the ingredient was one of the central puzzles of this experiment.

Preliminary preparations included making orpiment ash by three different methods, each documented in a separate video. See the [Alchemy Reconstruction]({{ '/alchemy-reconstruction/' | relative_url }}) page.
