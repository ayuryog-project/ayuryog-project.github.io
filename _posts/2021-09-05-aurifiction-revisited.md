We are nearing the end of this series of experiments. But before we wrap up with one last recipe, we want to revisit one we did before: a recipe for imitating gold from the *Rasaprakāśasudhākara*.

The recipe (chapter 11) calls for lead to be driven seven times into copper, and then for tuttha (blue vitriol / copper sulphate) to be driven seven times into that same copper. The result, according to the text, is a material that resembles gold. Our first attempt did not fully succeed, so this time we adjusted the proportions and technique.

Revisiting failed or partial experiments is, of course, central to the logic of experimental philology. The texts are terse. The first reading is rarely the right one.
