When Andrew and I selected recipes for this series of experiments, we favoured recipes that appeared simple. Method 14 for making silver from the *Rasaprakāśasudhākara* made the cut because it was short and featured only a few ingredients: copper, brass, bronze, arsenic sulfide, and silver. Nothing too fancy.

In practice, of course, the simplicity was somewhat illusory. Deciding what "driving" one metal into another (*vāhana*) means in operational terms — a common instruction in Sanskrit alchemical literature — is never straightforward. We discuss our interpretation in the video.
