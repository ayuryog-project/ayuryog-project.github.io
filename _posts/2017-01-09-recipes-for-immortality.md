This post accompanies an exhibition of *rasāyana* ingredients and alchemical apparatus mounted as part of the AyurYog project's public engagement activities. The exhibition brought together historical and contemporary material culture related to Indian and Tibetan longevity practices, allowing visitors to see — and in some cases smell — the substances described in Sanskrit and Tibetan alchemical texts.

What does it mean to make immortality recipes? The alchemical literature presents itself as thoroughly practical — these are instructions to be followed, not merely read. At the same time, the gap between recipe and outcome is wide, and the history of experimentation with these substances is one of persistent creative interpretation.
