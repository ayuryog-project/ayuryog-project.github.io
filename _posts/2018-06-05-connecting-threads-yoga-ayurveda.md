The twentieth century saw a dramatic intensification of the relationship between yoga and ayurveda — not only in India, where both traditions were being reformulated in response to colonialism and nationalism, but also in Europe and North America, where they were being adopted and adapted by new audiences.

This post traces key moments in that convergence, from the early nationalists who promoted both yoga and ayurveda as expressions of India's ancient scientific heritage, to the contemporary wellness industry in which the two are routinely packaged together. The history is more contingent and more contested than the seamless unity often presented in popular accounts.
