We are delighted to announce the publication of a special issue of *History of Science in South Asia*, edited by Dagmar Wujastyk, Suzanne Newcombe and Christèle Barois:

**Transmutations: Rejuvenation, Longevity, and Immortality Practices in South and Inner Asia**

The volume brings together contributions from scholars working across the history of yoga, ayurveda, rasaśāstra, and Tibetan medicine, and examines how these traditions have theorised and practised the extension of human life. The articles range from close analysis of Sanskrit and Tibetan primary sources to ethnographic and sociological perspectives on contemporary longevity practices.

The issue is freely available as open-access via the *History of Science in South Asia* journal website.
