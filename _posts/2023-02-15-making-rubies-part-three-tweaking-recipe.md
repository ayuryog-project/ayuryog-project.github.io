In our third attempt at producing artificial rubies from the *Rasaratnākara*, we introduced a technique borrowed from Graeco-Egyptian recipe literature: corroding and "opening up" stones to improve dye adhesion.

The *Rasaratnākara* is a thirteenth- to fifteenth-century alchemical treatise. Its *Vādakhaṇḍa*, chapter 19, describes the making of gems including rubies, sapphires, and emeralds. The recipes are terse, as is typical of Sanskrit alchemical literature, which means the experimenter must bring considerable interpretive judgement to bear.
