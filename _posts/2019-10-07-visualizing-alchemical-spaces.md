On the fifth-floor Science and Technology Heritage gallery of the National Science Center, Delhi, a small diorama shows some of the instruments used by South Asian alchemists. The diorama depicts a cluttered space full of *yantra*s (apparatuses) and ovens — a visual reconstruction of what an Indian alchemical laboratory (*rasaśālā*) might have looked like.

How do we visualise spaces of historical scientific practice? And how much can material culture — instruments, vessels, furnaces — tell us about the procedures performed in those spaces? This post reflects on representations of alchemical laboratories in museum displays, manuscript illustrations, and the Sanskrit textual tradition itself.
