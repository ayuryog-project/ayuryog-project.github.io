In the sixth procedure, the mercury was supposed to regain its power, which had been suppressed through the earlier cleansing procedures. With the seventh procedure, *niyamana* ('fixing' or 'restraining'), the reinvigorated mercury is stabilised so that it can be used productively in subsequent alchemical operations.

The *Rasahṛdayatantra* gives a characteristically brief description of the procedure, listing the necessary substances without explaining precisely how they are combined or applied. Working out the operative sequence from the text alone — and then testing that interpretation empirically — is the central challenge of experimental philology.
