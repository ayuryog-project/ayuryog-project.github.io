Indian alchemical texts portray their practitioners in varied ways. Some texts present the ideal alchemist as a *siddha* — a perfected being who has transcended ordinary human limitations through the combination of yoga, alchemy, and esoteric practice. Others depict a more mundane figure: a skilled craftsman (*śilpin*) or physician (*vaidya*) who uses alchemical methods primarily to produce medicinal formulations.

Who were the actual people who practised rasaśāstra? This post surveys the evidence from the textual tradition and from historical records, exploring the social and institutional contexts in which Indian alchemy was practiced.
