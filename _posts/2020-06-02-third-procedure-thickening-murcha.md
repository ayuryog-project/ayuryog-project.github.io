The third procedure in the *Rasahṛdayatantra*'s treatment of mercury is *mūrchā*, thickening or stupefaction. Its purpose is to remove the toxic properties of mercury using three plant-based substances: aloe, the three myrobalans (*triphala*), and leadwort (*Plumbago zeylanica*).

According to the text, mercury has three inherent faults: 'dirt', 'fire', and 'poison'. Aloe removes the first, triphala the second, and leadwort the third. The mercury must be thickened seven times with a mixture of these substances — an instruction whose practical meaning we worked out through repeated experiment.
