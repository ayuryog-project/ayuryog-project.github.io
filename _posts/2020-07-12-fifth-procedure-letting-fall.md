Nine months into this experiment, we have arrived at the fifth procedure: *pātana*, 'letting fall' or sublimation. This procedure is meant to rid the mercury once and for all of any residual amounts of tin and lead, which are understood as contaminants that render mercury poisonous.

The procedure involves preparing a copper-mercury paste and then conducting a condensation in a specially constructed clay apparatus. Some of the condensed mercury dropped onto the copper — an unexpected result that prompted us to reconsider our reading of the text's description of the device.
