We're back! Just for a one-off mini-series, though. Probably.

*Matsyakajjala* — fish black, or fish collyrium — is the essential base ingredient for producing a range of artificial gems according to the *Rasaratnākara*, a thirteenth- to fifteenth-century Sanskrit alchemical treatise. Its preparation involves burning dried fish in a controlled way to produce a fine black powder.

The experiment raises interesting questions about the sourcing of materials in historical alchemical practice, and about how much interpretive latitude a modern experimenter can take in reconstructing a recipe whose ingredients are sometimes uncertain or hard to obtain.
