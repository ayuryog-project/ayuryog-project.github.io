*Guest post by Keith Cantú*

One of the most important tasks in understanding any Sanskrit textual tradition is tracking the physical transmission of its manuscripts — where they were copied, where they ended up, and what variation exists between them. For Indian alchemical literature, this kind of geographical picture has rarely been attempted.

As part of my doctoral research and collaboration with the AyurYog project, I have begun mapping the manuscript witnesses of the *Rasaratnākara*, one of the longest and most complex Sanskrit alchemical treatises. The resulting map shows the distribution of known manuscript copies held in libraries and private collections across India, and raises interesting questions about the centres of alchemical learning and manuscript production.
