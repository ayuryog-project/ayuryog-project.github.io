The sixth procedure in the *Rasahṛdayatantra*'s treatment of mercury is *nirodhā*, normally translated as 'restraint' or 'suppression', but here understood as 'awakening' or 'reviving'. The mercury, whose activity has been suppressed by the foregoing cleansing procedures, is reinvigorated by burying it in a pot of salt water.

The gap between the ordinary meaning of *nirodhā* and its function here is a good example of the specialised vocabulary of Sanskrit alchemical literature, which regularly reassigns common terms to technical senses that must be inferred from context and tradition.
