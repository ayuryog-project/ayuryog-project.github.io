The two-day AyurYog workshop explored the historically linked South and Inner Asian practices of *rasāyana*, *kāyakalpa*, and *bcud len*. These are practices, treatment techniques, and formulations aimed at a variety of goals ranging from prolonging life, restoring youthfulness, and promoting physical health, to accessing special powers, achieving enlightenment, and attaining immortality.

Scholars working on Indian, Tibetan, and Nepalese sources gathered to compare methodological approaches and share results. The workshop produced a clearer picture of both the striking parallels between these traditions and the important differences that distinguish them — differences that are often obscured when they are presented as simply "Asian longevity practices."
