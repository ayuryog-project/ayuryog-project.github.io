We are very pleased to announce the newly published special volume of *Asiatische Studien / Études Asiatiques*: "Histories of Mercury in Medicine Across Asia and Beyond," edited by Dagmar Wujastyk.

Mercury holds a central place in Indian alchemical medicine — it is the defining substance of rasaśāstra — but its medical uses extend across Asia, from China to the Islamic world to early modern Europe. This volume brings together contributions examining mercury's role in medical traditions across a wide geographical and chronological range, asking what comparative perspectives reveal about the universal appeal of this remarkable and dangerous substance.

The volume is available from De Gruyter.
