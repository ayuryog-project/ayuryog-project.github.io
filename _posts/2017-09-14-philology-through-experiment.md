The year 2015 saw the beginning of two major European projects — one at the University of Vienna, the other based at SOAS in London — both focused on the study of texts describing practices belonging to the Sanskrit tradition. AyurYog and the Haṭha Yoga Project share a commitment to rigorous philological method and a willingness to ask what textual study can gain from experimentation.

In October 2015, members of both teams gathered for a workshop in Dorset. The occasion was the first attempt to recreate a procedure from the *Rasahṛdayatantra* under field conditions. This post reflects on what that experience revealed about the relationship between reading a text and doing what it describes.
