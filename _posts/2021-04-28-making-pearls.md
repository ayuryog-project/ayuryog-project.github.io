Well, this one is not exactly a charmer. It is, however, fairly straightforward: you make "pearls" out of fish eyes.

The recipe from the *Rasaprakāśasudhākara* instructs the alchemist to remove fish eyes, cook them in milk for a *yāma* (roughly three hours), then rub them with a black powder until they acquire a pearlescent surface.

The result is not intended to deceive a jeweller; it sits in the same category as the artificial coral and gems in this series — products that demonstrate a theoretical and practical command of material transformation rather than straightforward counterfeiting.
