*Abstract of a forthcoming article.*

The combining of yoga and āyurveda (Indian medicine) is unexceptional in the current global market for wellness. More than a married couple, yoga and āyurveda are deemed by many to be sisters, born of the same scriptural family, the Vedas. This current interplay raises two historical questions: how old might their relationship be, and was it as intimate in pre-modern times as it seems today?

This article approaches these questions through a systematic examination of shared terminology, theory, and practice across a selection of medieval yoga texts and āyurvedic sources. The evidence suggests that the relationship between yoga and āyurveda is real but complex: there are genuine shared frameworks and conceptual overlaps, but also significant divergences that resist easy synthesis. Understanding these entanglements precisely — rather than assuming a uniform ancient unity — is the task to which AyurYog is committed.
