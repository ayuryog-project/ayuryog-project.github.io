The *Rasahṛdayatantra*, the earliest of the Sanskrit alchemical works transmitted to us, is characterised by its concise style. With few flourishes, it succinctly describes alchemical operations in a structured way, providing a quick overview of procedures. However, the brevity of its descriptions also means that it can be difficult to fully understand the described procedures.

This post introduces our collaboration with Andrew Mason, a silversmith and metalworker who brings practical craft knowledge to the project. His expertise in working with metals and minerals has proved indispensable in translating terse Sanskrit instructions into workable experimental protocols — and in recognising when a procedure is going wrong.
