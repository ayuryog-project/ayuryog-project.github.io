The eleventh chapter of the *Rasaprakāśasudhākara* contains twenty recipes for making gold, improving the quality of gold, or imitating gold. The recipe of our current experiment is for making copper the colour of gold — aurifiction, not aurifaction.

The formula is deceptively simple: lead is driven into copper seven times, then copper sulphate (*tuttha*) is driven into the same copper seven times. The resulting material is said to resemble gold. Our first round did not yield the result we had hoped for. Nor did the second. But the failure is itself instructive.
