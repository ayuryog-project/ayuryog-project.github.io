An entire week of public events at the Centre of Yoga Studies at SOAS (25–29 March 2019) was organised around a two-day workshop titled "Disciplines and Dialogue: The Future of Yoga and Ayurveda Studies," convened by Suzanne Newcombe (AyurYog/Inform/Open University) and Karen O'Brien-Kop (SOAS).

Among the highlights was a presentation by Dr Louis Komjathy on Daoist alchemy, which opened productive comparative perspectives between the Indian and Chinese traditions. Both traditions developed sophisticated theories of material transformation in the service of longevity and spiritual perfection; both used mercury as a central substance; and both employed a vocabulary that moves fluidly between the material, the physiological, and the cosmological. The similarities and differences are instructive for thinking about what "alchemy" means cross-culturally.
