With the colour and texture of our first batch of artificial coral not quite right, we embarked on a second (and actually also third) round of experiments.

The recipe from the *Rasaprakāśasudhākara* (chapter 11, verses 138–140) calls for purified conch shell ground to a fine powder, combined with a range of other ingredients and processed with heat. The coral-red colour that should result proved elusive.

In the second and third attempts we varied the firing temperature and duration, and experimented with the ratio of colouring agents. The results were still not identical to natural coral, but they were substantially closer — and the process of adjustment revealed a great deal about what the text is trying to describe.
