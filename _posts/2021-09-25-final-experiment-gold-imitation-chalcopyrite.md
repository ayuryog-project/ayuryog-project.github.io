We have come to the final experiment of this series!

Chalcopyrite — a copper iron sulphide mineral with a brassy, gold-like lustre — features in a recipe for gold imitation that differs from the other *aurifiction* recipes in one important respect: it does not require smelting the ingredients in a furnace. This made it an intriguing practical and interpretive puzzle to reconstruct.

The experiment is drawn from the *Rasaprakāśasudhākara*, a sixteenth-century Sanskrit alchemical treatise. See the accompanying video on the [Alchemy Reconstruction]({{ '/alchemy-reconstruction/' | relative_url }}) page.
