The AyurYog project is now underway. In this first substantial post from the Inform team, I reflect on what it means to begin untangling the histories of yoga, āyurveda, and rasaśāstra — and on the pleasures and difficulties of working across disciplinary boundaries.

One early experience captures the spirit of the project well: a workshop with metalworker Andrew Mason in which I tried, under his instruction, to melt zinc — one of the steps in preparing a zinc *rasāyana* according to the alchemical texts. Moving from reading recipes to attempting them, even in a simplified form, changes how you read. The text suddenly has weight, temperature, smell. And the gaps in the recipe — the things the author assumed the reader already knew — become newly visible and newly puzzling.
