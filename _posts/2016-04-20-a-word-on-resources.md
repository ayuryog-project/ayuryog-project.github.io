In new research projects, one sets out to cover new ground, explore the unknown, make exciting discoveries, and set the record straight on some previous assumptions. With this focus on discovering the new, it is easy to forget just how much research depends on work already done by others.

This post takes a moment to acknowledge some of the key secondary literature and reference works that AyurYog has relied on, including Dominik Wujastyk's *The Roots of Āyurveda* (Penguin), Michio Yano and others' ongoing work in the history of Indian science, and Arion Roşu's foundational contributions to the study of āyurvedic and alchemical texts. Good research builds on the shoulders of predecessors — and naming them is part of scholarly integrity.
