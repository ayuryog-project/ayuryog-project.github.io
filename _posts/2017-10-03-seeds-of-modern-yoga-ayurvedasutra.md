The *Āyurvedasūtra* is a relatively late Sanskrit text that explicitly brings together āyurvedic and yogic frameworks. Its full significance for understanding the modern synthesis of yoga and medicine has not yet been widely appreciated by scholars.

This post introduces the text and argues that it represents an important moment in the textual history of the yoga–ayurveda relationship — one that anticipates many of the claims made in contemporary wellness culture about the unity of the two traditions. Understanding this text on its own terms, rather than reading it through the lens of modern categories, is essential for getting the history right.
