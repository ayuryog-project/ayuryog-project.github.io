The figure of the teacher (*guru*, *ācārya*) is central to both yoga and āyurvedic traditions. In classical texts, the transmission of knowledge is depicted as inseparable from the relationship between teacher and student — a relationship bound by ethical obligations on both sides. But what does this mean in practice, and how has the role of the teacher changed as these disciplines have spread globally?

This post draws on textual and historical evidence to examine how teachers have been represented in Sanskrit sources, and reflects on what those representations might mean for contemporary practitioners and scholars.
