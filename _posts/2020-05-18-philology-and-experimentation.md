The *Rasahṛdayatantra*, the earliest of the Sanskrit alchemical works transmitted to us, is characterised by its concise style. With few flourishes, it succinctly describes alchemical operations in a structured way. However, the brevity of its descriptions also means that it can be difficult to fully understand the procedures described.

This is where experimental philology comes in. By attempting to recreate the described procedures, we can test our textual interpretations against physical results. When our first attempt at steaming mercury (*svedana*) produced a result that did not match the text's stated outcome, we knew we had to revise our reading.

This post introduces the methodology behind the reconstruction series and explains how we think about the relationship between Sanskrit text and laboratory practice.
