The terms used for the alchemical procedures sometimes evoke the language of āyurvedic therapy. *Svedana*, the "steaming" of the first alchemical procedure, has a correlate in āyurvedic treatment, where it refers to the application of heat to the patient's body to induce sweating. The alchemical *svedana* borrows this term and applies it to mercury — suggesting that the text's authors thought of mercury as something analogous to a living body that could be purified through therapeutic procedures.

The second procedure, *mardana* (trituration, or grinding), continues this therapeutic logic. Mercury is ground with a combination of substances — molasses, burnt wool, salt, soot, brickdust, and mustard — in sour gruel, for three days.
