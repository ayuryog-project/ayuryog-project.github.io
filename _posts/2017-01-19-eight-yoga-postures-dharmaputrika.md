The *Dharmaputrikā* ("Little Daughter of Dharma") is an early yoga manual that AyurYog has been studying for its medical dimensions. Chapter Ten, on *yogacikitsā*, has been discussed elsewhere on this blog. But the text also contains a description of eight yoga postures (*āsana*) that is of considerable historical interest.

This post presents and analyses those eight postures, examining how they relate to later haṭhayoga classifications and what they reveal about the state of āsana practice in the text's milieu — an interesting counterpoint to the image in the Los Angeles County Museum of Art that illuminates this post.
